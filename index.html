Infant Mortality from 2016 -2020

Infant mortality is any death of a person under the age of 1 (UNICEF). This dashboard will explore how infant mortality varies worldwide from 2016-2020. With this initial discovery, we get to what typically high infant mortality is commonly associated with and explore the impact Covid-19 has on infant mortality.

Global Overview

Map of the Infant Mortality Rate

From the map above, we can see that Africa had the highest infant mortality normalised values throughout the period. The other area of higher values was in the area surrounding India. But otherwise, the remaining countries had similar rates of infant mortality. It is also essential to know that there weren't massive amounts of changes for throughout the time period.

Infant Mortailty Over Time by Continent

Looking at the continents' infant mortality, we see that Asia & Africa have the highest number of infant mortality. For this area chart as well, we can see from 2016 to 2020 that overall, there has been a decrease in infant mortality rates over the last couple of years, which is a good thing to see, particularly from those countries in Africa & Asia.

What does a high Infant Mortality tell us about GDP and Life Expectancy

Infant Mortality by GDP Per Capita

From a GDP per capita front, we can see another similar result: the higher the GDP per capita amount typically results in a lower infant mortality rate. We can also see that smaller countries, in particular, appear to have large GDP per capita values and fewer deaths, which is what we expect since they would have fewer infants borns and fewer people to divide the countries' GDP.

Infant Mortailty by Life Expectancy

When comparing life expectancy and infant mortality, we can see that a higher infant mortality rate typically means a lower life expectancy. This scatter plot also reinforces what was discussed above, as Asian and African countries have higher infant mortality numbers.

COVID-19 Impact

The impact of COVID-19 on Infant Mortality Rate

Looking above, we can see that during the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, there was an increase in the number of infant mortalities as a rate. So, we can now see a slight increase in the infant mortality rate during the covid pandemic's first year. So, even though the number of infant mortalities went down, the rate increased. This is concerning, probably due to the strain placed on the healthcare system.
